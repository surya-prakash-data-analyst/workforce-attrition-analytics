# Business Impact Analysis

## Financial Cost of Attrition

At an industry-standard replacement cost of 2× monthly salary per departing employee:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Employees who left | 238 |
| Avg monthly income (attrited) | $4,813 |
| Estimated cost per departure | $9,626 |
| **Estimated annual replacement cost** | **$2,291,000** (~$2.3M) |
| At 3× salary (senior roles) | ~$3.4M |

These figures are conservative — they exclude productivity loss during vacancy, onboarding time for replacements, team disruption costs, and institutional knowledge loss.

---

## Revenue Risk by Segment

### Sales Department — Highest Priority
- 93 employees left from Sales (39% of all attrition)
- Attrition rate: 20.7%
- Sales reps at 39.3% — losing revenue-generating staff has direct top-line impact
- Each Sales Representative departure = direct pipeline loss + ramp time for replacement (typically 6–12 months to full productivity)

### Overtime Employees — Systemic Risk
- Employees working overtime churn at 30.6% vs 10.4% for those without
- Overtime is the single strongest behavioral predictor of attrition in this dataset
- Burning out high-commitment employees is a self-defeating policy

### Low-Salary Band ($0–$5K) — Volume Risk
- 753 employees in this band (51% of total workforce)
- Attrition rate: 21.6% — highest of all salary bands
- 163 of 238 departures came from this band

## Retention Program ROI Estimates

| Program | Cost Estimate | Attrition Reduction | Annual Savings |
|---------|--------------|---------------------|---------------|
| Overtime policy reform (cap + pay) | $200K/year | 40-50% OT attrition reduction | ~$460K |
| Sales retention program | $150K/year | Reduce Sales from 20.7% to 13% | ~$370K |
| Sub-$5K compensation review | $500K/year | Reduce low-salary attrition 25% | ~$320K |
| 18-25 mentorship program | $80K/year | Reduce young-employee attrition 30% | ~$280K |
| **Total investment** | **~$930K** | **Combined 30% overall reduction** | **~$690K net savings** |

A 30% overall reduction in attrition (from 16.1% to ~11%) would save approximately **$690,000 annually** after program costs — a positive ROI in year one.

---

## Intangible Impact

Beyond replacement costs, high attrition damages:

- **Team morale** — Remaining employees notice and question their own decisions to stay
- **Customer relationships** — Sales and customer-facing roles leaving disrupts continuity
- **Employer brand** — High attrition gets noticed on Glassdoor and through industry networks
- **Institutional knowledge** — Employees with 5+ years of tenure carry process knowledge that takes years to rebuild

The data shows retained employees average 7.4 years at the company vs 5.1 years for those who left. Each departure that could have been prevented represents significant accumulated knowledge walking out the door.
